Career

In 2001, Shooter Jennings went to Los Angeles and joined a rock band named “Stargunn.” Here, he sang as a frontman and also played the piano. By the end of 2001, Shooter and other “Stargunn” artists released their first album, “The Only Way Up Is Down.” In 2002, when his father died, he sang the song, “I’ve Always Been Crazy,” as a tribute to him. On 30 March 2003, Shooter left “Stargunn” and began to work as a solo singer.

In 2005, in a contract with Show Dog-Universal Music, he released his first album, “Put the “O” Back in Country.” The album became so famous that it got the 22nd position in the “Hot Country Songs” list released by Billboard. On 4 April 2006, the second solo album of Shooter called “Electric Rodeo” was released. On 23 October 2007, his third album, “The Wolf,” was released. On 24 March 2009, Shooter’s first compilation album (and fourth in total) called “Bad Magick: The Best of Shooter Jennings and the .357’s” was released.

After the release of this album, Shooter had fulfilled the contract of releasing four albums with Universal records and, therefore, left it. After leaving Universal, the name of Shooter’s band was changed from “The .357’s” to “Hierophant.” The fifth album of Shooter called “Black Ribbons” was released on 20 March 2010 with his band “Hierophant.” By the end of 2010, a movement called “XXX” was launched by Shooter and many other artists. The aim of the movement was to assist musicians who wrestle to get mainstream identification.

On 13 March 2012, Shooter released his sixth album, “Family Man,” in a contract with E1 Music and The Triple Crown. On 31 July 2012, he appeared in the first single singer, Bucky Covington, titled “The Drinking Side of Country.” When the album, “Family Man” was being recorded, a part of it was trimmed and inserted in the then-upcoming album of Shooter: “The Other Life.”

With the help of filmmaker Blake Judd, Shooter released a 32-minute horror movie, “The Other Life.” It was based on his album of the same name and played six songs from the album. The shooter also entertains on a radio show called “Electric Rodeo” on “SiriusXM’s Outlaw Country station.” The show airs at night every Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday and focuses mainly on rock, country, and outlaw music.

Awards and Achievements

As a singer, Shooter Jennings has not received any award to date, but many of his tracks have been in the list of Billboard’s Hot country songs. In 2001, “Music Connection” magazine listed Shooter as one of the “Hot 100 Unsigned Artists” of 2001. His short movie, “The Other Life,” won the “Best Short Film” award at Horror Hound Festival of 2013.
