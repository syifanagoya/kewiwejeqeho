Ash Soan Full Overview and Wiki

Early Beginning

Ashley Ash Soan’s journey as a British drummer is a tale of talent, versatility, and collaboration. Educated at the Grove School in Shropshire and subsequently at Salford University and Drumtech, Ash honed his skills early on.

His career took flight when he joined Del Amitri in 1994, marking the beginning of an illustrious journey in the music world.

Career Highlights and Collaborations

Ash’s time with Del Amitri, until 1997, was just the start. He then collaborated with Faithless, showcasing his adaptability across music genres.

The turn of the millennium saw Ash with Squeeze, further solidifying his position as a formidable drummer. His versatility shone through varied session work, accompanying artists like Will Young, Lisa Stansfield, and Rick Wakeman.

Influences and Musical Style

Ash’s drumming style, influenced by Stewart Copeland and Steve Gadd, among others, is notable for its unique blend of precision and flair.

His ability to adapt to different musical styles made him a sought-after session musician, contributing to recordings with Adele, CeeLo Green, and even the Robbie Williams and Gary Barlow single Shame.
